### 1. Misty Coastal Shores & Beaches (Entry / Survival Biome)

- **Description**: Fog-bound rocky beaches, tidal pools, and jagged cliffs where most outsiders first arrive (via shipwreck). Constant mists hide dangers like Blight-twisted sea creatures and illusory mirages.
- **Key Features**: Shipwreck graveyards, hidden coves, healing springs blessed by Eldath.
- **Campaign Role**: Book 1 starting area; survival challenges, first meetings with Lirael and Sylva.
- **Elemental Tie**: Water's Lament and Shadow's Veil harmonies begin here.

### 2. Shadowed Marshes & Swamps (Blight-Corrupted Lowlands)

- **Description**: Vast, fog-choked wetlands with twisted mangroves, necrotic pools, and sinking ruins. The Blight is strongest here, spawning undead and corrupted wildlife.
- **Key Features**: Haunted Marsh-like areas (inspired by Fincayra's Haunted Marsh), ogre hoards, hidden song-sites.
- **Campaign Role**: Major exploration in Books 1–2; Grimthar's domain, Blight horde battles.
- **Elemental Tie**: Water's Lament, Shadow's Veil, and Earth's Bind (muddied and corrupted).

### 3. Enchanted Groves & Ancient Forests (Fey-Touched Woodlands)

- **Description**: Lush, bioluminescent woodlands with talking trees, dryad groves, and massive ancient oaks (echoing Arbassa from Fincayra). Fey portals flicker, and nature spirits thrive.
- **Key Features**: Druma Wood-inspired glades, Heartstone Grove (central sanctuary), herbalist villages.
- **Campaign Role**: Safe havens for allies (Lirael, Elowen); trials of spirit and nature in Books 1–2.
- **Elemental Tie**: Spirit's Call and Light's Dawn harmonies; core of natural balance.

### 4. Volcanic Rifts & Fiery Highlands (Draconic Core)

- **Description**: Steaming lava fields, black ash plains, geothermal vents, and towering calderas. Rivers of molten rock and fire elementals roam freely.
- **Key Features**: Drakthar's buried lair, Flame Trial sites, obsidian forges.
- **Campaign Role**: Book 3 focus; volcanic uprisings, rage trials, dragon climax.
- **Elemental Tie**: Flame's Roar harmony; source of inner/outer fire mastery.

### 5. Aerial Spires & Floating Peaks (Sky-Touched Heights)

- **Description**: Towering cloud-piercing rock spires, wind-swept plateaus, and levitating stone islands held by ancient magic. Storms rage constantly, with aerial currents and flying creatures.
- **Key Features**: Storm giant citadels, rift portals, nesting grounds for harpies/wyverns.
- **Campaign Role**: Books 2 (Wind's Whisper), 4–5; storm rifts, faction quests, final armada battle.
- **Elemental Tie**: Wind's Whisper harmony; origin of Stormwings (permanent flight boon).

### 6. The Haunted Mirror Veil (Illusory / Shadow Realm Overlay)

- **Description**: Not a strict biome but a warping overlay—mists that shift into deceptive realms of mirrors, echoes, and alternate realities. It bleeds into other biomes, especially marshes and highlands.
- **Key Features**: Echo Realms (false fates), shadow familiars, Queen Vespera's sanctum.
- **Campaign Role**: Book 4 core; illusionary conflicts, truth-shattering trials.
- **Elemental Tie**: Shadow's Veil harmony; tests deception vs. truth.

### 7. Highland Enclaves & Elven Peaks (Elevated Sanctuaries)

- **Description**: Cooler, windswept highlands with terraced valleys, crystal-clear lakes, and elven ruins. Less corrupted, serving as refuges.
- **Key Features**: Ancient elven watchtowers, Light's Dawn song-site.
- **Campaign Role**: Mid-to-late game hubs; ally strongholds, prophecy revelations.
- **Elemental Tie**: Light's Dawn and Earth's Bind (stable stone).
